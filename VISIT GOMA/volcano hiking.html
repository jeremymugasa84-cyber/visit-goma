Hiking Nyiragongo Volcano

Experience the thrill of climbing
an active volcano

From 150$ per person

Overview

Stand at the edge of the world's largest lava lake atop Mount Nyiragongo one of the most active volcanoes on Earth. Rising 3,470 metres above sea level inside Virunga National Park, just 21 km from Goma, this is not just a hike. It is a once in a lifetime experience. You will trek through dense tropical rainforest, cross volcanic rock fields, spot wildlife along the way, and reach the summit just in time to witness the breathtaking glow of molten lava lighting up the night sky.

Important Info

  • Starting point: Kibati Ranger Post, 30 min from Goma
  • Duration: Full day + overnight at summit
  • Difficulty: Challenging (but no experience needed)
  • Min age: 15 years old
  • Best season: June to September (dry season)

What to Bring

  • Warm jacket — it gets very cold at the summit
  • Rain jacket
  • Sturdy hiking boots or rubber shoes
  • Enough drinking water
  • Packed lunch for the ascent
  • Headlamp or torch for night at the crater
  • Valid passport and Congo visa
  • Yellow fever vaccination card
